## About

Healthcare fraud is one of the most aggressively prosecuted categories of federal crime. The Department of Justice, HHS Office of Inspector General, and FBI work through the Medicare Fraud Strike Force and other task forces to identify and prosecute healthcare providers who defraud federal health programs.

## Scale of Healthcare Fraud

The DOJ annually recovers billions in healthcare fraud through:
- Criminal prosecutions resulting in prison sentences
- Civil settlements and judgments
- False Claims Act qui tam lawsuits

In fiscal year 2023 alone, DOJ recovered over $2.2 billion in healthcare fraud cases. The Medicare Fraud Strike Force has charged thousands of defendants with fraudulent billing exceeding $30+ billion.

## Common Healthcare Fraud Schemes

### Billing for Services Not Rendered
The most basic healthcare fraud: submitting claims for services, tests, or procedures that were never performed.

### Upcoding
Billing for more expensive services than were actually provided — for example, billing a complex procedure when only a simple service was rendered.

### Kickbacks
Receiving or paying illegal remuneration in exchange for patient referrals. Anti-Kickback Statute violations are among the most common healthcare fraud charges.

### Prescription Drug Fraud
Illegally prescribing, dispensing, or diverting controlled substances. Pill mill prosecutions, pharmacy fraud, and prescription drug trafficking are heavily prosecuted.

### Home Health Fraud
False certifications of homebound status, billing for unneeded services, and kickback arrangements with referring physicians.

### DME Fraud
Durable medical equipment fraud involves billing for wheelchairs, orthotics, CPAP machines, and other equipment that was never needed, never ordered, or never delivered.

### Telehealth Fraud
Post-COVID explosion in telehealth fraud involving sham telehealth companies that bill Medicare for unnecessary services without genuine patient contact.

## Investigating Healthcare Fraud

Healthcare fraud investigations involve:
- **Data analytics**: Identifying unusual billing patterns
- **Undercover operations**: Agents posing as patients or recruiters
- **Whistleblowers**: Qui tam relators filing False Claims Act suits
- **Audits**: CMS and OIG billing audits

## Federal Penalties

Federal healthcare fraud convictions carry significant penalties:
- Up to 10 years per count of healthcare fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1347)
- Additional charges for conspiracy, false statements, kickbacks
- Loss of medical licenses and exclusion from federal programs
- Asset forfeiture of fraud proceeds
